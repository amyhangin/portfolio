Anna May Wong
黄柳霜

The first Chinese-American Hollywood actress

Anna May Wong's life in a timeline:

  • 1906 - Born in Los Angeles, California
  • 1910 - The second eldest child out of seven, attended a public school with her older sister. However, they soon transferred to a Presbyterian Chinese school due to racial bullying
  • 1915 - Started begging filmmakers to give her roles, so they called her "C.C.C.", "Curious Chinese Child"
  • 1921 - Dropped out of Los Angeles High school, pursues a full-time acting career, landed her first screen credit for Bits of Life
  • 1923 - Played her first leading role in the early Metro two-color Technicolor movie The Toll of Sea
  • 1924 - Continued to be assigned stereotypical exotic Asian supporting roles
  • 1928 - Left Hollywood for Europe for to search for more respectable roles
  • 1929 - Played a starring role in her last silent film in the UK, Piccadilly, which was a sensation, albeit because of racial laws, interracial couples were forbidden. Therefore, she could not have any on-screen kisses with her white costars
  • 1930 - Returned to Hollywood, refused a stereotypical role playing a Japanese character
  • 1931 - Took on her last stereotypical role in Daughter fo the Dragon alongside the only other well-known Japanese-American actor, Sessue Hayakawa. She was also underpaid compared to her male costar and white male costar
  • 1934 - "The World's Best-Dressed Woman", voted by the Mayfair Mannequin Society of New York
  • 1936 - After MGM refused to cast Wong as the leading female role, a Chinese character, in The Good Earth, Wong travels to China to study her fatherland
  • 1938 - Aided Chinese refugees by auctioning off her movie costumes and donated the money
  • 1942 - Donated the salary she earned from Bombs over Burma and Lady from Chungking to United China Relief
  • 1945 - Invested in real estate, owning multiple properties in Hollywood
  • 1949 - Returned to acting after a six-year absence, the same year of her father's passing at age 91
  • 1956 - Left apartment house managing and moved in with her brother in Santa Monica
  • 1960 - The first Asian-American actress to receive a star at the Hollywood Hall of Fame
  • 1961 - Died at age 56 in Santa Monica, California from a heart attack

"It's a pretty sad situation to be rejected by Chinese because I'm 'too American' and by American producers, because they prefer other races to act Chinese parts."

--Parish 1976, p. 536.
